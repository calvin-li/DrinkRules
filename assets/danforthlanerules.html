1. Cups are located 12-18 inches from the back edge of the table

2. If playing doubles, cups are on the center line, if fours, cups are centered in the respective quadrant (beer stains on table should help with location).

3. Players are not allowed to strike the ball until it has passed the cups (also known as the no defense rule).

4. If the ball hits the cup, person whose cup was hit takes one sip.

5. If the ball lands in the cup, person whose cup the ball is in drinks all remaining beer in cup. Ball cannot be removed first.

6. Player stays on table until someone puts a ball in their cup (or they pass out).

7. Cannot hit opponents cup, or put in opponents cup on serve.

8. Ball is in play unless it has hit a cup or gone in a cup, or in the net.

9. Play continues until ball is in play. There is no limit as to the number of bounces (the ball can even roll off the table and be hit on the bounce off the floor).
